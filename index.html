I am making chainmail without forging, you can too! The materials are cheap, and the tools are simple.

First: spool the wire around the dowel using a drill.

Second: remove the spool and cut into rings with

Third: Weave! Find a pattern online that matches what you want to make.

The navigation bar can lead you to the tools I am using as well as my progress and projects.
Galvinized Wire
I am using 14 Gage Galvinized wire, wich can be found at any hardware store. There exists 10 and 16 gage versions, that are thinner and thicker respectively, I reccommend 14 gage as a nice middle ground. Wire can also be found under "Electric Fence Wire".
Wire Cutters
I am using a Kobalt handheld wire cutter. I would reccomend another pair that locks not at the handle, as I have found that the Kobalt lock will interfere with the cutting. Otherwise, any hand held wire cutter is what I would reccommend, as sometimes the rings will get stuck and need to be pulled apart.
MISC
You will need a drill that can hold a 1/2 in. dowel (any variety of either is fine). You will need to drill a hole wider than the wire to spool it around. You will need 2 pairs of pliers as the spooled and cut rings will not be able to be set and opened with bare hands. Optionally: a stand/weave bar for creating chainmail sections.
